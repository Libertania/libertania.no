Open Source Cities:
Online Collaboration

The Libertania Project is a joint effort to build a knowledge base of publicly open and freely available documentation and instructions for planning, building and running a new city or town.

All documentation is available on Github and anyone is free to contribute. The online forum is available for discussions and questions from the community.

The project is dependent on the expertice, knowledge and experience from You, and hundreds of others.

Agile Development:
Human Centered

The world needs a lot more cities to be built all around the world. The majority of new cities are built with top-down management, with expensive planning and heavy construction.

The Libertania Project aims to have a balance between bottom-up and top-down approaches to planning and processes. Allowing for a more agile approach that enables cities to grow in a more naturnal and organic manner.

The planning and building should revolve around the humans that inhabit the spaces. Humans should not adapt to spaces, but spaces should be built with humans in mind.

Modern Cities:
The Technology Factor

Technology is the main driving force that allows the creation of modern self-sustained open source cities.

It is the technological advances in computers, alternative energies such as solar, home automation, electric transporation, low-energy utilities, energy storage and the ability for humans to connect through the Internet to share expertice and knowledge, that makes it feasable to realize these cities.

Cities:
Liberstad

One of the first cities to be built using The Liberterian Project blueprint for open source cities.

Liberstad is being built in South Norway. City is currently in planning and financing state.
